Project Deep Dive

Competitive Market Analysis — FMCG

Problem Statement
What was the business question?

A mid-size FMCG brand (simulated) needed to understand its competitive position within a saturated market. The goal was to identify where competitors were gaining share, what consumer segments were being underserved, and what pricing strategies were driving growth.

Methodology
How was it approached?
  • Collected secondary data from industry reports (Statista, IBEF, Nielsen)
  • Built a competitive benchmarking matrix across 5 brands on 12 KPIs
  • Applied SWOT and PESTLE frameworks to contextualize data findings
  • Performed market sizing using TAM–SAM–SOM model
  • Created a Python-based data pipeline to clean and normalize datasets
  • Visualized findings using Matplotlib and Seaborn

Project Deep Dive

HR Attrition & Workforce Analytics Study

Problem Statement
What was the business question?

A simulated mid-size IT services firm was experiencing a 21% annual attrition rate — significantly above the industry benchmark of 13%. HR leadership had no analytical framework to identify which departments, salary bands, or tenure groups were highest-risk. This project built one from scratch.

Methodology
How was it approached?
  • Sourced IBM HR Analytics dataset (1,470 employee records) for realistic simulation
  • Used Excel Power Query to clean, reshape, and standardize raw HR data
  • Built pivot tables segmenting attrition by department, role, age band, and salary tier
  • Applied conditional formatting heatmaps to flag high-risk cohorts instantly
  • Created Excel-based attrition rate calculator with dynamic slicers
  • Designed a 4-view Tableau dashboard: Overview, Risk Matrix, Tenure Analysis, Compensation Gap
  • Used Tableau parameters for interactive "what-if" salary adjustment scenarios

Key Insight

Sales employees in the low salary band with 1–3 years tenure had a 34% attrition rate — 2.6× the company average. A targeted ₹15K salary adjustment for this cohort was projected to reduce overall attrition by 8%, saving approximately ₹4.2M annually in replacement costs.
